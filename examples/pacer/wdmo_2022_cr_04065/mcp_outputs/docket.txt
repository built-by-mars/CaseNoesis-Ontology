09/12/2024   74 ORDER denying motion to continue 73 as to Bryan C. Perry (1). Continuances are disfavored and should be granted only for a compelling
                reason. United States v. Wright, 682 F.3d 1088, 1090 (8th Cir. 2012) (citation omitted). Among the factors the Court considers in ruling on
                Defendant's motion to continue are: "(1) the nature of the case and whether the parties have been allowed adequate timing for trial
                preparation; (2) the diligence of the party requesting the continuance; (3) the conduct of the opposing party and whether a lack of
                cooperation has contributed to the need for a continuance; (4) the effect of the continuance and whether a delay will seriously
                disadvantage either party; and (5) the asserted need for the continuance, with weight to be given to sudden exigencies and unforeseen
                circumstances." United States v. Cordy, 560 F.3d 808, 815-16 (8th Cir. 2009) (citation omitted).

                 Here, after reviewing each factor, the Court denies Mr. Perry's motion. First, while this case does involve 41 pending counts against Mr.
                 Perry, the third superseding indictment was filed in October 2023, almost 12 months ago (Doc. 48). Mr. Perry is currently in custody
                 awaiting trial. (Doc. 10). Second, this is the fourth motion to continue in this case. (See Docs. 22, 43, 61). The last time the Court granted
                 a continuance, the Court noted that "[a]bsent extraordinary circumstances, no further continuances shall be granted." (Doc. 61).

                 Third, the Court has not been made aware of any improper conduct or lack of cooperation by the opposing party.

                 Finally, the Court will address the fourth and fifth factors together. Here, the Court has not been made aware that any party would be
                 seriously prejudiced by proceeding to trial on October 28, 2024. Further, the Court finds the requested need for the continuance
                 unpersuasive. In his motion, counsel for Mr. Perry seeks additional time based on multiple conflicts, both professional and personal, that
                 necessitate a continuance.(Doc. 73). The Court finds that counsel has not identified any "sudden exigencies and unforeseen
                 circumstances." Cordy, 560 F.3d at 815-16.

                 Based on these observations, the Court concludes that this matter may proceed to trial on October 28, 2024, without affecting Mr. Perry's
                 constitutional rights.
