             added via superseding indictment. Defendant further represented that he may file a motion to sever. To avoid any wasteful duplication of

             forthcoming motion to sever. When such motions are resolved, the Court will amend or vacate the Pretrial Scheduling Order as necessary.

             joint motion regarding conflicts at 1:30 PM ET on JUNE 20, 2025 in Courtroom 28A.

             consent to tolling. In light of the additional complexity in this case occasioned by the superseding indictment, the need for defense counsel
             to review additional discovery and coordinate with counsel for co-defendants, and the pending and forthcoming motions regarding
             vacatur, severance, and conflicts, the Court finds that the ends of justice served by excluding Speedy Trial time on the original indictment
             outweigh the interests of Defendant and the public in a speedy trial. 18 USC 3161(h)(7)(A). Accordingly, the Court will exclude the time
             between May 12, 2025 (the date this hearing was originally scheduled) and June 20, 2025, from the speedy trial clock on the original
             indictment. As to the superseding indictment, Defendant's speedy trial time is tolled to June 20, 2025 in parallel with his co-defendants. 18

             Defendants Ferro, Flansburg, and Tangeman were present via Zoom with their consent. Counsel for all defendants were present in person.

             The Court began by discussing the status of discovery productions, noting that it received the report by the Coordinating Discovery
             Attorney. The Government reported that there are some remaining electronic devices that have yet to be downloaded by the FBI cart unit;
             the Government added that it does not have the data from these devices but knows that they are in FBI custody. The Government
             explained that this data has not yet been downloaded because the Government wanted to get a better idea of how many Defendants
             anticipated proceeding to trial before requesting a download, as it could request an expedited process with a more limited number of
             devices. The Government reported that it now has a better idea of which cases will be reaching a resolution short of trial and will talk to
             the FBI case agent to get a timeline of when the information from these devices can be downloaded, estimating that it will likely take close
             to 90 days.

             pleas.

             discuss any potential resolutions short of trial, discovery productions, and determining a schedule for trial. During this discussion, the
             Government reported that there is a possibility that it will be able to consolidate the Defendants into one group before trial.

             Ferro, Flansburg, Mehta, and Tangeman each consented to the exclusion of time until the next status hearing. In light of the unusual
             complexity of this matter occasioned by the number of co-defendants, the breadth of the charged conspiracy, and the volume of discovery
             (including substantial phone and computer extractions); the Court finds that the ends of justice served by excluding Speedy Trial time until
             the next status hearing outweigh the interests of Defendants and the public in a speedy trial and that it would be unreasonable to expect
