4, Victim B was a 76-year-old woman and a resident of the State of Washington.

B. THE CONSPIRACY:

Beginning at a time unknown to the Grand Jury but at least by February 9, 2022, and
continuing until at least April 4, 2022, in the Eastern District of Louisiana and elsewhere, KEEL
and PANCHAL, and others known and unknown to the Grand Jury, did knowingly and willfully
combine, conspire, confederate, and agree, to devise a scheme to defraud individuals of money
and property by making false material representations and omissions regarding their identity and
the safety of their funds, and in furtherance of this scheme to defraud, did make or cause to be
made, interstate wirings, in violation of Title 18, United States Code, Section 1343.

Cc. SCHEME TO DEFRAUD:

1. It was part of the scheme and artifice to defraud that co-conspirators contacted
victims by phone and unlawfully represented themselves to be employees or officers of the United
States to persuade victims to entrust their money with them.

2. It was further part of the scheme and artifice to defraud that co-conspirators traveled
by plane to meet with victims and fraudulently obtain money from them.

3. It was further part of the scheme and artifice to defraud that co-conspirators caused
interstate wirings to occur when victims performed financial transactions at co-conspirators’
behest to obtain and transfer money to them.

D. MANNER AND MEANS:

1. It was part of the conspiracy that co-conspirators contacted Victim A and Victim B
by phone and portrayed themselves to be federal agents. Co-conspirators falsely represented to
victims that the victims’ bank accounts were implicated in criminal activity. Co-conspirators

further misrepresented to victims that, in order to preserve their funds, victims had to withdraw
Case 2:22-cr-00115-ILRL-KWR Document 28 Filed 10/13/22 Page3of8

their money from these accounts and entrust their funds with co-conspirators for purported
safekeeping.

2. It was further part of the conspiracy that co-conspirators directed victims to transfer
funds via mail, via Green Dot cards, and by hand in person-to-person meetings. Green Dot cards
are a service of Green Dot Bank, which is based in Utah.

3. It was further part of the conspiracy that, on or about February 9, 2022, Victim B’s
computer appeared to have been hacked, and she was directed to contact a phone number
purporting to be a software company for assistance. Victim B called the phone number and was
then told by co-conspirators that her bank accounts had been implicated in criminal activity. Co-
conspirators subsequently contacted Victim B and falsely portrayed themselves as U.S.
Department of Justice agents. They informed Victim B that she was a suspect in purported criminal
activity and that her accounts were in danger of being seized.

4. It was further part of the conspiracy that, beginning on or about February 10, 2022,
Victim B mailed approximately $140,000 of her funds to co-conspirators at their direction to avoid
her funds from being seized by purported authorities. On or about February 24, 2022, Victim B
met with co-conspirators to hand over $60,000 at their direction. On or about March 10, 2022,
KEEL and PANCHAL flew to Washington to meet with Victim B to obtain from her an additional
$100,000 under the same false pretense of being federal agents. After meeting with Victim B on
or about March 11, 2022, and obtaining a box containing $100,000 in cash from her, KEEL and
PANCHAL departed the state. In total, Victim B withdrew $300,000 from her bank accounts and

transferred those funds to KEEL, PANCHAL, and other co-conspirators at their behest.

5. It was further part of the conspiracy that, beginning at least in March 2022, co-
conspirators contacted Victim A by phone and represented themselves to be U.S. Department of
Treasury agents. They falsely informed Victim A that her bank accounts were implicated in fraud
and money laundering. Co-conspirators contacted Victim A by phone from locations outside of
Louisiana.

6. It was further part of the conspiracy that, beginning on or about March 7, 2022,
Victim A transferred approximately $60,000 via Green Dot cards to co-conspirators at their behest.
Victim A’s purchase of Green Dot cards at the direction of co-conspirators caused a wire
transaction from the Eastern District of Louisiana to a location outside of Louisiana.

7. It was further part of the conspiracy that, in or around April 2022, KEEL and
PANCHAL contacted Victim A again by phone and told her that she needed to withdraw more
money in cash and hand it over to them under the pretense that they were U.S. Department of
Treasury agents. Victim A complied, and she followed the perpetrators’ instructions in
withdrawing an additional $60,000 from her bank.

8. It was further part of the conspiracy that, on or about April 2, 2022, KEEL and
PANCHAL flew from the State of Washington to New Orleans International Airport for the
purpose of fraudulently obtaining funds from Victim A.

9. It was further part of the conspiracy that, on or about April 3, 2022, Victim A went
to a location in Hammond, Louisiana, at the instructions of KEEL and PANCHAL. She met with
KEEL and handed over to him the additional $60,000 that she had withdrawn from her bank

account at their instruction.

10. ‘It was further part of the conspiracy that, on or about April 4, 2022, KEEL and
PANCHAL contacted Victim A again and instructed her that she needed to get all her money out
of her accounts because these funds may be seized, and that she must give that money to them for
safekeeping. They told her to meet at the same location in Hammond, Louisiana, to hand off the
money.

11. It was further part of the conspiracy that, on or about April 4, 2022, KEEL went to
the same location in Hammond, Louisiana, to meet with Victim A and receive the additional funds
that she withdrew from her account.

All in violation of Title 18, United States Code, Section 1349.

COUNT 2
(False Personation of an Employee or Officer of the United States)

A. AT ALL TIMES MATERIAL HEREIN:
The allegations in Section A and D of Count 1 are incorporated as though fully set forth
herein.

B. THE OFFENSE:

On or about April 4, 2022, in the Eastern District of Louisiana, the defendants,
CHRISTOPHER L. KEEL and JAYESH J. PANCHAL, did falsely assume and pretend to be
an officer and employee of the United States acting under the authority thereof, that is an agent of
the U.S. Department of Treasury, and in such assumed and pretended character did act as such, in
that they falsely stated that they were agents of the U.S. Department of Treasury and that Victim
A must entrust her money to them, in violation of Title 18, United States Code, Sections 912 and
