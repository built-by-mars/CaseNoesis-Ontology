FACTUAL BASIS

Defendant CHRISTOPHER L. KEEL (“KEEL”) has decided to enter a plea of guilty in
this case. Should this matter proceed to trial, the United States would prove beyond a reasonable
doubt, through credible testimony and reliable evidence, the following facts. Unless stated
otherwise, the following acts occurred within the jurisdiction of the Eastern District of Louisiana.

Arrest of KEEL in Hammond, Louisiana

On April 4, 2022, the Tangipahoa Parish Sheriffs Office received information via a formal
complaint that a 77-year-old woman, hereinafter “Victim A”, who is a resident of Hammond, LA,
was the victim of fraud being perpetrated by individuals portraying themselves as agents with the
US. Department of Treasury. Victim A reported that she had thus far given the perpetrators
approximately $120,000, and that they were contacting her to give them more money.

According to Victim A and her adult son, Victim A had been contacted by phone in the
early weeks of March 2022 by the perpetrators. The person on the phone informed her that he was
a U.S. Department of Treasury agent and that her bank accounts were implicated in fraud or money
laundering. The perpetrator told Victim A that she should withdraw money from her bank
accounts, purchase Green Dot cards, which she should then transfer to the so-called “agents.” The

“agents” would then hold Victim A’s money in safekeeping and return it to her once the trouble

Search of KEEL’S Cell Phone and Fraud Against Victim B in Washington State and
Victim C in Louisiana

During the course of this investigation, agents searched KEEL’S phone pursuant to a
search warrant. Agents found various photos and messages with other co-conspirators. One
photograph that was sent by KEEL to another co-conspirator depicted a box with the name and
address of a woman from Washington State (“Victim B”). The box appeared to have been labeled
by Victim B as having come from her. The photo appeared consistent with other photos on
KEEL’S phone whereby KEEL sent photographs of boxes to other co-conspirators to confirm
receipt of money from victims.

Agents identified Victim B and contacted her. Victim B informed agents that she was 76
years old, and her husband was ill with cancer. She told agents that she had been defrauded of
$300,000. Victim B said that, on February 9, 2022, her computer reported a pop-up virus and she
had to call “Microsoft” at a phone number, which she did. The person at the other end (an unknown
co-conspirator) warned her that her bank accounts may have been involved in suspicious activity.
She was then contacted by two women in February 2022 and told that they were federal agents
with the Department of Justice, and that her bank accounts had been implicated in criminal activity.
They told Victim B that she needed to transfer her money to them for safekeeping. Victim B
explained that she mailed approximately $140,000 in cash via UPS to these “agents” at their
direction. Victim B kept all receipts, and she kept a journal in which she noted all of the
transactions and details about them.

The co-conspirators continued to contact Victim B to demand more money under pretenses
that they were federal agents. On February 24, 2022, Victim B met with an unknown woman and.

gave her $60,000 by hand. She met with the woman at the Target parking lot at South Center Mall

Additional Evidence on KEEL’S Cell Phone

Text messages on KEEL’S phone showed that KEEL had been communicating with co-
conspirators since at least October 2021. On October 22, 2021, KEEL texted an individual saved
as “King K” on his cell phone. KEEL informed King K that KEEL had “two calling each day
ready to work.” KEEL asked King K to provide work because otherwise “I’mma loose them
because they was ready for me and I give them my word that it’Il be some work[.]” King K replied
the next day that “work resumes next week” and “hopefully we will have some work in coming
week for all of us.” King K wrote to KEEL that “then again 1 runner changed his mind and stopped
working since 9th September, so we had to stop work...now having some small things...but
expecting more to come next week”. These text messages show that KEEL is aware that he is part
of a larger conspiracy. KEEL also has contact information saved on his phone for an individual
located in India, which agents have learned is the headquarters of this conspiracy.

Furthermore, KEEL texted photographs of packages on prior occasions, similar to the
photograph of Victim B’s package. KEEL’S cell site data also showed that, when KEEL texted
these photos of packages, KEEL was located in Texas and Oklahoma at times when Panchal’s
phone was in New York, showing that KEEL worked with other co-conspirators to execute this
scheme to defraud.

As of the time of this plea, agents have determined that since October 22, 2021, members
of the conspiracy have been responsible for $4,498,327 in actual and attempted losses to 31 victims

located in the United States.

Sentencing Stipulation

For the purposes of sentencing only, the parties agree that KEEL is responsible for

Limited Nature of Factual Basis

This proffer of evidence is not intended to constitute a complete statement of all facts
known by KEEL, and it is not a complete statement of all facts described by KEEL to the
government. Rather, it is a minimum statement of facts intended to prove the necessary factual
predicate for his guilty plea. The limited purpose of this proffer is to demonstrate that there exists

a sufficient legal basis for KEEL’S plea of guilty to the charged offenses.
